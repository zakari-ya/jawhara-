# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are local customers in Chichaoua and nearby areas who want to discover Boulangerie & Patisserie Jawhara, check the bakery's offer, build trust quickly, and contact the business through WhatsApp, phone, Facebook, or Google Maps.

Secondary users are event and cake buyers planning birthdays, weddings, family gatherings, or custom dessert orders. They need confidence that the bakery can handle special occasions with care, quality, and clear contact paths.

The business owner or client is also an important handoff user. The site should feel presentable, finished, and easy to maintain as a professional brochure website.

## Product Purpose

This site exists to present Jawhara as a trustworthy local bakery and patisserie, show its products and occasion services, and turn interested visitors into direct WhatsApp or phone conversations.

Success means the first screen immediately communicates bakery craft and credibility, product sections are easy to scan in Arabic and French, review and business details stay honest, and every major section gives visitors a natural path to contact or visit the bakery.

## Brand Personality

Jawhara should feel premium, warm, and crafted. The voice is respectful, confident, and local: proud of quality and Moroccan bakery tradition without sounding exaggerated or artificial.

Arabic is the primary experience, with French as a polished secondary language. Copy should feel human, clear, and client-ready in both languages.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like a generic AI-generated landing page, a luxury template disconnected from a real local bakery, or an inflated ecommerce brand with unverifiable claims.

Avoid vague hype, fake urgency, unsupported "best in town" language, fake review wording, placeholder business details, decorative clutter that hides the food, and features the business does not offer such as checkout, payment, accounts, analytics dashboards, or a CMS.

## Design Principles

Make the bakery real first: show actual products, real contact paths, honest review numbers, and clear location details before decorative flourish.

Earn trust quietly: use restrained confidence, clean bilingual copy, consistent metrics, and production-ready metadata instead of exaggerated claims.

Lead with appetite and craft: imagery, texture, and spacing should make fresh bread, Moroccan sweets, and occasion cakes feel tangible and desirable.

Respect bilingual flow: Arabic RTL should feel native and primary, while French should remain elegant and complete rather than translated as an afterthought.

Keep conversion simple: every main journey should end in WhatsApp, phone, Facebook, Google Maps, or a clear product/contact section, with no unnecessary interaction layers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA where practical for a brochure site: readable contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus, meaningful image alternatives, semantic landmarks, and clear button/link labels.

Support Arabic RTL and French LTR consistently. Motion should remain subtle and non-essential, and future animation work should respect reduced-motion preferences.
